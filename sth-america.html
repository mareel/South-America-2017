Macchu Picchu - Sulkantay trek

A five day trek starting in Cusco, where the altitude first makes itself known! What a beautiful city surrounded by mountains and historic reminders of the incas and their peers. A chance to try some Peruvian food, without alcohol, to avoid feeling even more under the weather! The cobblestone central square is a beautiful, contemplative space both day and night.

The trek commences with an early pick up from our hotel and a 3 hour drive to our drop off point. Our party numbered 4 of the 11 trekkers who were from Toronto, Canada.

>Day 1 was the most difficult as we climbed through 5000 meters. It was cold and damp at the camp and most were eager to crawl into their sleeping bags with the hope of feeling better for day 2.

Day 2 to 4

An early morning bus took us up to Macchu Picchu and we arrived before 6am to a foggy vista. As the fog dissapated we were treated to views of the ruins all around us. We toured the ruins for several hours as the heat of the day built up with the crowds.This city was a truly epic undertaking by the Incas and is surrounded by magnificent beauty.

Brazil

Rio de Janeiro

We cycled around the tourist part of the city from Copacabana to Ipanema, visited Christ the Redeemer on Sugarloaf Mountain and looked over a favella.

Iguacu Falls

We visited the falls from both the Brazilian and Argentinian sides. I think the Argentinian side was more spectacular where we did the speedboat ride under the waterfall and walked along the waterfall over boardwalks.
